How to Use Burkflow Builder

A quick reference for getting the most out of your build tracker.

Welcome

Welcome to Burkflow Builder! This tool helps you plan and track your custom home build from start to finish — budgets, selections, timeline, inspections, and everything in between. Take a few minutes to read through this page so you get the most out of it.

Sample Data: See How It Works

When you first open Burkflow Builder, you'll see a 'Smith Residence' example project filled with sample numbers, selections, contractors, and tasks. This is sample data — it's there so you can explore the app, see how the pages work together, and get ideas for how to organize your own project before you start.

⚠️ Important: Don't Edit the Sample Project

The Smith Residence sample is for exploration only. Do not start entering your real project information into it.

Why? When you eventually turn Sample Data OFF to start your own project, the sample content is replaced — any changes you made to it will not carry over.

Instead: Browse the sample to learn how the app works, then turn Sample Data OFF first, and enter your own project from there.

WHEN YOU'RE READY TO START YOUR OWN PROJECT:

Look for the 'Sample Data' toggle (you'll see it in the app). Turn it OFF to clear out all the example content and start with a clean project.

  • Toggling Sample Data OFF wipes the example content and replaces it with empty fields, ready for your own information.
  • Toggling Sample Data ON brings the example back if you want to reference it again.
  • Your real data and the sample data are kept separate — turning sample mode on won't erase work you've already started, and turning it off will bring your work back.

TIP: Spend 10-15 minutes browsing the sample project first. Click into rooms, look at how budget items link to selections, see how the timeline is organized. It's much easier to understand the app this way than reading instructions cold.

How Your Data Is Saved

Your work saves automatically as you go — there's no Save button to remember. Everything lives right in this file on your computer, so your information stays private and you don't need an account, internet connection, or subscription to use it.

To keep your data safe and accessible across devices, just use the Export feature (covered in the next section) — think of it like saving a Word document. It's quick, easy, and gives you total control over your information.

Saving Backups & Using on Multiple Devices

At the bottom-left of the sidebar, you'll find the Export / Import button. This is your best friend.

EXPORT: Click Export to download a backup file (.json) of everything you've entered. Save it anywhere you'd save an important document — cloud storage like Google Drive or Dropbox, a folder on your computer, or even email it to yourself.

IMPORT: Open Burkflow Builder on any device, click Import, and select your backup file. All your data appears instantly, exactly as you left it.

Recommended habit: Export a backup at the end of each work session. It takes about 5 seconds and means you'll always have an up-to-date copy.

Bonus tip: You can use Burkflow Builder on multiple computers (your laptop at home AND your desktop at the office, for example) by exporting from one and importing to the other whenever you want them in sync. Burkflow Builder works best on a laptop or desktop screen.

Attaching Documents, Photos & Receipts

Throughout Burkflow Builder, you'll see paperclip icons (📎) where you can attach receipts, contracts, photos, warranty documents, inspection certificates, and more. Instead of uploading files directly into the app, Burkflow Builder uses LINKS to documents stored in your own cloud storage. Here's why this is actually better for you:

  • Your files stay in YOUR cloud storage (you own and control them)
  • The app file stays small and fast
  • You can access the same documents from any device
  • No size limits

HOW TO ATTACH A DOCUMENT OR PHOTO:

Step 1: Upload your document or photo to a free cloud storage service. Any of these work great:

  • Google Drive (free with any Google account)
  • Dropbox
  • OneDrive
  • iCloud Drive
  • Any other cloud service that gives you shareable links

Step 2: Get a shareable link to your file.

  • In Google Drive: right-click the file → 'Share' → 'Get link' → make sure it's set to 'Anyone with the link can view' → click 'Copy link'
  • In Dropbox: hover over the file → 'Share' → 'Copy link'
  • Other services have similar 'Share' or 'Get link' options

Step 3: In Burkflow Builder, click the paperclip icon (📎) on the item you want to attach to. Paste your link into the field. Click Save.

That's it! You can attach multiple links to the same item (e.g., multiple receipts for one purchase, or several photos of one inspection). Click the paperclip again to add more, edit existing links, or remove them.

CLICKING A LINK:

Once attached, clicking the paperclip and then 'Open' on any link will open the document in a new browser tab.

ORGANIZING YOUR CLOUD STORAGE:

For best results, create a dedicated folder for your build project. A simple structure works well: 

📁 My Home Build
   📁 Receipts
   📁 Contracts & Bids
   📁 Inspection Photos & Certificates
   📁 Warranty Documents
   📁 Selection Photos

Then just upload your files into the appropriate subfolder and link them in Burkflow Builder.

TIP: Take photos with your phone, then upload them to your Google Drive or Dropbox app. Once they're in the cloud, you can grab a shareable link and paste it into Burkflow Builder from your computer.

Tips for First-Time Owner Builders
  • Set up your Budget first. Enter your allowances before you start making selections.
  • Use the Selections page to track what you're actually buying. Link items to Budget allowances and watch the totals update automatically.
  • Inspections happen in a specific order. The list comes preloaded — just fill in dates as you schedule them.
  • Export a backup at least once a week. Your data lives in your browser, not in the cloud.
